About


Dataset

Features

The data set that we used includes information of Hass avocado sales from January 2015 to November 2020 throughout the United States. For each recorded date, the average price, total volume sold, total number of bags of each type sold, and the region were provided.



Goal

Our Mission

We are attempting to enable our project, the Avocado Value Optimizer (AVO), to learn from past data and trends to accurately predict avocado prices. AVO will help sellers, manufacturers, and consumers all around. Sellers in particular will be able to identify the optimal price for avocados and maximize their exports. Consumers will benefit from locating the cheapest areas to purchase avocados.



Analysis

Our Observations

The price of an avocado usually fell between $0.44 and $3.25 with an overall average price around $1.40. There was a mean of 850,644 avocados sold in each region. Lastly, the region that sold the most avocados was the West, selling an average of 3,215,323 avocados each day.

Exploration

Visualization of our Data

We analyzed the heat map and tried to choose factors that had high correlation with each other. However, average price had a weak correlation with all the factors.



Using a line graph, we were able to see the fluctuation of prices throughout the years and see a visual of the prices as well as the general trend associated with it.



Using a bar chart with long formatted data, we tracked the amount of bags of avocados that each region in the U.S. acquired per year, and we were able to identify a significant increase in demand for avocados.



Using a bubble plot and a grouped data set, we were able to identify the price ranges in which companies sold the most number of bags of produce.

Conclusion

Our Results

From analyzing our models, we discovered that avocado prices usually rise from the start of spring to mid-summer. We also discovered that the coastal areas, especially the west, of the United States usually had the highest prices of avocados. Our models also show a correlation between price and volume: as the number of avocados sold increases, the price drops. Lastly, as time goes on, prices slightly increase, probably due to inflation, and are likely to remain above $1.25.
